# 🎒 Packing List — Argentina Dec 2026 / Jan 2027

**4 friends · 26 nights · Southern Hemisphere summer**

This trip crosses **four climate zones** in a single journey — pack smart.

---

## Climate by Region

| Region | Dates | Conditions | Temp Range |
|--------|-------|------------|-----------|
| Buenos Aires | Dec 9–13, Jan 3 | Hot, humid, sunny | 26–35°C |
| Puerto Iguazú | Dec 11–12 | Very hot, tropical, humid | 28–38°C |
| Puerto Madryn | Dec 14–16 | Mild, coastal winds | 15–25°C |
| Ushuaia | Dec 17–19 | Cool, windy, unpredictable | 6–16°C |
| El Calafate / El Chaltén | Dec 20–24 | Cold, very windy, possible rain | 5–18°C |
| Bariloche | Dec 26–31 | Mild–warm, mountain thunderstorms possible | 12–26°C |
| Mendoza | Jan 1–2 | Hot, dry, sunny | 28–36°C |

> **The Patagonia Rule**: Weather can go from blazing sun to horizontal rain in 20 minutes. Layers are not optional.

---

## 🧳 Luggage Strategy

- **Domestic flight limits**: 23 kg hold + 8 kg carry-on (Aerolíneas Argentinas)
- **Recommended setup**: 1 × 55–65 L main bag + 1 × 25–30 L daypack per person
- The daypack doubles as your hiking pack in El Chaltén and Bariloche
- Consider packing Patagonia gear at the **top** of your bag — you'll need it immediately after Iguazú

---

## 👕 Clothing

### Hot Weather (Buenos Aires, Iguazú, Mendoza)
- [ ] 3–4 × lightweight T-shirts / short-sleeve tops
- [ ] 1–2 × shorts
- [ ] 1 × casual dress / smart-casual outfit (for tango shows, winery lunches)
- [ ] 1 × light long-sleeve shirt (sun protection on winery bikes)
- [ ] Underwear ×7+ (quick-dry preferred)
- [ ] 1 × swimwear (Iguazú boat safari; optional hotel pools)

### Patagonia Layers (Madryn, Ushuaia, Calafate, Chaltén, Bariloche)
- [ ] 2 × moisture-wicking base-layer tops (merino wool ideal)
- [ ] 1 × thermal/fleece mid-layer top
- [ ] **1 × windproof + waterproof hard-shell jacket** ← **most important item**
- [ ] 1 × packable down or synthetic puffer jacket (can double as mid-layer)
- [ ] 2 × hiking / trekking trousers (convert to shorts useful)
- [ ] 1 × warm hat / beanie
- [ ] 1 × lightweight gloves
- [ ] 1 × buff / neck gaiter (Patagonian winds are brutal)
- [ ] 2 × warm socks (merino wool preferred)
- [ ] 2–3 × regular socks

### All-Region Basics
- [ ] 1 × light rain poncho (Iguazú will soak you — a poncho is better than a jacket there)
- [ ] 1 × compact umbrella (Buenos Aires and Mendoza)

---

## 👟 Footwear

- [ ] **1 × waterproof hiking boots / trail shoes** (essential for El Chaltén 20 km hikes and glacier trek)
- [ ] 1 × comfortable walking shoes or sneakers (Buenos Aires, Mendoza sightseeing)
- [ ] 1 × sandals / flip-flops (Iguazú, hostel showers, Mendoza)

> One pair of waterproof trail runners can cover both hiking and casual use.

---

## 📄 Documents & Money

- [ ] **Passport** — valid through July 2027 at minimum
- [ ] Copies of passport (digital + 1 physical copy stored separately)
- [ ] Travel insurance policy (number + emergency contact) — printed and on phone
- [ ] All flight and accommodation confirmation emails (offline access)
- [ ] **USD cash in pristine condition** — no tears, pen marks, or heavy creases. $100 bills preferred. Bring more than you think you need.
- [ ] Credit/debit card (Visa/Mastercard widely accepted; carry 2 different cards)
- [ ] Small wallet for daily cash; leave main cash in hotel safe

> **El Chaltén has NO ATM.** Withdraw cash in El Calafate before boarding the bus.

---

## 📱 Electronics

- [ ] Smartphone + charger cable
- [ ] Power bank (10,000–20,000 mAh — essential for long hiking days)
- [ ] Universal travel adapter (Argentina uses Type I plugs — 3-pin angled)
- [ ] Camera (optional — modern smartphones are excellent; DSLRs great for wildlife)
- [ ] Earphones / earbuds
- [ ] E-reader or tablet (buses and downtime)
- [ ] Download offline Google Maps for: Buenos Aires, Puerto Madryn, Ushuaia, El Calafate, Bariloche, Mendoza

---

## 💊 Health & Pharmacy

- [ ] **Altitude sickness tablets** (acetazolamide / Diamox) — consult your doctor; may be relevant in Mendoza/high-altitude areas
- [ ] Ibuprofen / paracetamol
- [ ] Antihistamines
- [ ] Anti-diarrhoeal (Imodium) — change in diet and water
- [ ] Antiseptic wipes and small plasters
- [ ] Blister prevention (compeed patches, bodyglide)
- [ ] High-SPF sunscreen (SPF 50+) — UV is deceptively intense in Patagonia and Mendoza
- [ ] Insect repellent (essential in Iguazú; useful in Patagonia)
- [ ] Lip balm with SPF (wind-drying in Patagonia)
- [ ] Motion sickness tablets if needed (boat tours at Iguazú and Beagle Channel)
- [ ] Personal prescription medications + carry-on letter from doctor

## 🥾 Activity-Specific Gear

### El Chaltén Hikes (Laguna de los Tres, Laguna Torre)
- [ ] Trekking poles (highly recommended for the steep final push to Laguna de los Tres)
- [ ] 25–30 L daypack with hydration sleeve or space for 1.5 L water
- [ ] Snacks / trail food for 6–8 hr hike (buy in El Calafate — limited shops in Chaltén)
- [ ] Headlamp (Dec = near-24 hr daylight, but useful for early starts)

### Perito Moreno Ice Trek
- [ ] Crampons and equipment are **provided** by the operator
- [ ] Wear: waterproof pants + warm base layer + waterproof jacket
- [ ] Waterproof gloves (not ski gloves — you need dexterity)

### Iguazú Boat Safari
- [ ] Waterproof bag / dry bag for phone and camera
- [ ] Change of clothes (you WILL be completely soaked)

### Wildlife Watching (Península Valdés, Ushuaia)
- [ ] **Binoculars** — essential for whale watching, elephant seals, and birds
- [ ] Zoom lens if bringing a camera

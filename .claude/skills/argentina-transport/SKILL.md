# Argentina Transport Guide

## When to Use
- Planning how to get between Argentine cities or regions
- Choosing between flights and buses for a given route
- Navigating Buenos Aires on arrival (airport to hotel)
- Booking domestic transport (flights, long-haul buses)
- Using Buenos Aires public transport on a daily basis
- Travelling between Argentina and Uruguay by ferry

## Procedure

### Step 1: Identify the Journey
Establish:
- **Origin and destination**
- **Time available** (budget travellers often accept 18-hr buses; business/luxury opt for flights)
- **Budget** (buses are 1/5–1/10th the cost of domestic flights)
- **Luggage** (large packs are better on buses; no baggage fees)

### Step 2: Consult the Route Guide
Load [transport-guide.md](./references/transport-guide.md) for:
- Domestic airline options (Aerolíneas Argentinas, Flybondi, JetSMART, LADE)
- Key long-haul bus routes and journey times
- Buenos Aires city transport (SUBE card, metro, buses, taxis, ride-share)
- Ferry services to Uruguay
- Regional transport quirks (Patagonia, Northwest)

### Step 3: Make the Recommendation
Apply the decision framework:

| Route Distance | Time Available | Recommendation |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| < 300 km | Any | Bus (3–5 hrs), comfortable and affordable |
| 300–800 km | Flexible | Bus semi-cama or cama preferred (5–12 hrs overnight) |
| 300–800 km | Limited | Fly (1–2 hrs) |
| > 800 km | Any | Fly unless budget-constrained; overnight bus viable |
| Patagonia circuits | Flexible | Mix of flights + buses (El Calafate↔El Chaltén by bus; Bariloche↔El Calafate by air) |

### Step 4: Provide Booking Guidance
- Domestic flights: book at www.aerolineas.com.ar, www.flybondi.com, www.jetsmart.com; also Despegar.com (Argentine Expedia equivalent)
- Long-haul buses: book at www.plataforma10.com.ar (aggregator) or www.omnilineas.com; also Flechabus, Andesmar, Cata International websites directly
- Ferries: www.buquebus.com (Buenos Aires ↔ Colonia / Montevideo)
- Buenos Aires metro + bus: Buy a SUBE card (rechargeable) at any kiosk; recharge at kiosks or SUBE app

### Step 5: Flag Practical Caveats
- Book domestic flights 4–8 weeks ahead for Jan–Feb and Jul school holiday peaks
- Aerolíneas Argentinas is state-owned and prone to industrial action (strikes) — have a backup plan for critical connections
- Long-haul buses are genuinely comfortable (lie-flat cama class) — not budget-backpacker only
- Retiro terminal (Buenos Aires): Enormous 3-building complex; confirm which building your company uses

## Adapting for Other Destinations
The decision framework (distance/time/budget) and bus-vs-flight logic apply to any South American country. Substitute local airlines and bus companies.

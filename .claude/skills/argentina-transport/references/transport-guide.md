# Argentina Transport Guide

## Buenos Aires Airports

### Ezeiza — Ministro Pistarini International Airport (EZE)
- International arrivals and intercontinental flights
- Located 35 km southwest of Buenos Aires centre (~45–75 min drive depending on traffic)
- **Transfer options to city**:

| Option | Journey Time | Cost (approx) | Notes |
|--------|-------------|---------------|-------|
| **Tienda León bus** | 45–70 min | USD 15–20 | Best value; runs frequently; drops at Madero and Constitución/Av. 9 de Julio; buy ticket at arrivals hall |
| **Remise (pre-booked taxi)** | 45–90 min | USD 30–45 | Safer than street taxis; book at official remise counters in arrivals |
| **Uber / Cabify** | 45–90 min | USD 25–40 | Must exit to departures level or designated area; can be lower than remise at off-peak times |
| **UBER / InDriver app** | Same | Similar | App-based; pay by card; safer than street hail |
| **Public bus (Línea 8)** | 90–120 min | Pesos (very cheap) | Requires SUBE card; not practical with luggage |

**Avoid**: Unlicensed taxi touts in arrivals hall — use official counters or pre-booked apps only.

### Aeroparque — Jorge Newbery Airport (AEP)
- Domestic flights + regional international (Brazil, Uruguay, Chile, Paraguay, Bolivia)
- Located 5 km north of city centre in Palermo; accessible by bus or taxi
- **Transfer options**:
  - Taxi/Uber: 15–25 min to Palermo/Recoleta, USD 8–15
  - Bus Línea 33, 45, 160: Cheap; requires SUBE card; 20–30 min

## Domestic Airlines

### Aerolíneas Argentinas (AR)
- State-owned; most comprehensive network; flies to 40+ domestic destinations
- **Best for**: Reliability (mostly), loyalty program, baggage allowance
- **Watch out for**: Prone to pilot/worker strikes (multiple per year); always have a Plan B; check AR social media before travel
- **Baggage**: 23 kg check-in, 8 kg carry-on standard economy
- Book: www.aerolineas.com.ar or app

### Flybondi (FO)
- Low-cost carrier; growing network; significantly cheaper than Aerolíneas
- **Best for**: Budget-conscious travellers on popular routes (BA–Bariloche, BA–Mendoza, BA–Salta)
- **Watch out for**: Extra fees for checked baggage (must add at booking); less flexible cancellation
- Flies from Jorge Newbery (AEP) and also El Palomar (EPA) — check which airport
- Book: www.flybondi.com

### JetSMART (JA)
- Chilean LCC operating in Argentina; routes similar to Flybondi
- **Best for**: Price competition on key routes; often cheapest for Buenos Aires–Patagonia
- Book: www.jetsmart.com

### LADE (Military Aviation)
- State airline serving remote Patagonian towns with no other air service
- **Destinations**: Comodoro Rivadavia, Río Gallegos, Santa Rosa, Viedma, etc.
- **Book**: Walk-in offices or www.lade.com.ar — limited online booking; sometimes cash only at airport
- **Tickets**: Fill up fast; book as far ahead as possible

### Key Domestic Routes and Approx Flight Times

| Route | Time | Airlines | Notes |
|-------|------|----------|-------|
| Buenos Aires (AEP) → Bariloche | 2 hrs | AR, FO, JA | Scenic arrival into Andes |
| Buenos Aires (AEP) → El Calafate | 3 hrs | AR, FO | No direct Bariloche–El Calafate; fly via BA |
| Buenos Aires (AEP) → Ushuaia | 3.5 hrs | AR, FO | Most southerly scheduled service in world |
| Buenos Aires (AEP) → Mendoza | 1.5 hrs | AR, FO, JA | Also reachable by bus (14 hrs cama, overnight) |
| Buenos Aires (AEP) → Salta | 2 hrs | AR, FO | Also bus (22 hrs cama — popular overnight) |
| Buenos Aires (AEP) → Iguazu (IGR) | 2 hrs | AR, FO | |
| Bariloche → El Calafate | 1.5 hrs | AR | Key Patagonia link |
| El Calafate → Ushuaia | 1 hr | AR | Key Patagonia circuit link |
| Salta → Iguazu | 1.5–2 hrs | AR | Sometimes via Buenos Aires |

**Booking tip**: Check both AEP and the new budget hub El Palomar (EPA, 35km from centre) — Flybondi often uses EPA; factor in transfer time.

## Long-Distance Buses (Ómnibus de Larga Distancia)

Argentine long-haul buses are among the best in South America. Top class (cama ejecutivo) = fully flat seats, meal service, wine, sometimes steak.

### Bus Classes

| Class | Recline | Features | Best For |
|-------|---------|----------|---------|
| Común/Semi-cama | 130–145° | Basic recliner | Short trips <6 hrs |
| Cama | 150–160° | Legrest, meal, snacks | 8–14 hr journeys |
| Cama ejecutivo | 175–180° (flat) | Meal, wine, blanket, individual TV | 14–22 hr journeys |
| Suite / Cama suite | Fully flat (single or double) | Premium meal, cocktails, bed | Very long or luxury |

**Cama ejecutivo** is comparable to business class on a short-haul flight — highly recommended for Buenos Aires → Salta (22 hrs) or Buenos Aires → Bariloche (22 hrs).

### Key Companies
- **Flecha Bus**: Premium; excellent cama service; nationwide network
- **Andesmar**: Good for Mendoza, Cuyo, northwest routes
- **Cata Internacional**: Chilean/Argentine company; good for Mendoza → Santiago
- **El Rápido**: Good network
- **Via Bariloche**: Bariloche and Lake District specialist
- **Don Otto / ETAP**: Patagonia routes

### Retiro Terminal (Buenos Aires)
- Main long-haul bus hub in Buenos Aires; 3 buildings: Retiro I, Retiro II, Retiro III
- Confirm which building your company departs from
- Located adjacent to Retiro train station and San Martín railway
- Safe inside terminal; exercise caution on surrounding streets at night
- Luggage storage (consigna) available; ticket offices per company

### Booking Buses
- **www.plataforma10.com.ar**: Best aggregator — see all companies + prices
- **www.omnilineas.com**: Similar aggregator
- Direct company websites (Flecha Bus, Andesmar etc.)
- Ticket machines and counters at Retiro terminal (cash and card)
- Book popular routes (BA → Mendoza on Friday evening) 1–2 weeks ahead

### Key Bus Routes and Times

| Route | Duration | Company Options | Notes |
|-------|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Buenos Aires → Mendoza | 14–16 hrs | Andesmar, Flecha Bus | Excellent overnight cama option |
| Buenos Aires → Salta | 20–22 hrs | Andesmar, Flecha Bus | Long but cama ejecutivo is comfortable |
| Buenos Aires → Bariloche | 20–22 hrs | Via Bariloche, Andesmar | Night bus; arrive morning |
| Buenos Aires → Córdoba | 8–9 hrs | Many options | Several departures daily |
| Buenos Aires → Rosario | 4 hrs | Many options | Half-hourly service |
| El Calafate → El Chaltén | 3 hrs | Chaltentravel, Caltur | Only connection; ~3 daily in season |
| El Calafate → Río Gallegos | 4 hrs | Various | For LADE flights south |
| Salta → Jujuy | 2 hrs | Many options | Frequent service |
| Salta → Cafayate | 4 hrs | Various | Scenic quebrada route |
| Mendoza → Santiago (Chile) | 7–8 hrs | Cata, Turbus, Bus Sur | International; check border crossing status |

## Buenos Aires City Transport

### SUBE Card
- Rechargeable card for metro (subte), city buses (colectivos), suburban trains, and ferries
- **Buy at**: Any kiosk (quiosco) or correo (post office); also some pharmacies; cost ~50 pesos
- **Recharge at**: Kiosks, SUBE official machines, SUBE app (online)
- **Use**: Tap on boarding for bus, tap at entry for metro
- **Tip**: Load at least 1,000 pesos at start of stay; running out mid-ride is inconvenient

### Metro (Subte)
- 6 lines: A, B, C, D, E, H
- Covers main commercial/tourist areas: Palermo (D line), Recoleta (D), San Telmo (E), Microcentro (B, C, D), Once (A, H)
- Runs: ~5:30am–10:30pm (weekdays); shorter on weekends
- Fare: flat rate per trip, cheap in USD terms
- Not 24-hour — late nights require taxis or Uber

### Buses (Colectivos)
- Extensive network of 140+ lines; reaches everywhere the metro doesn't
- Apps: **Moovit** (best for trip planning, shows real-time arrivals), **Cuándo Viene** (local app for arrival times)
- Board at front, tap SUBE, exit from rear door
- Fares vary slightly by distance zone
- Runs 24 hours (though frequency drops 1–5am)

### Taxis and Ride-Share
- **Official remises**: Booked by phone or app; safest option
- **InDriver / Cabify**: Most recommended app-based options in Argentina; payment by card; safer than flagging street taxis at night
- **Uber**: Operating in Buenos Aires (grey zone legally but widely used); payment by card
- **Street taxis (radioTaxi)**: Yellow and black; legal taxis have a taxímetro (meter) — insist it is used; tip: only take RadioTaxi or Remis called by your hotel at night

### Trains (Suburban)
- 7 suburban rail lines from city-centre terminals (Retiro, Constitución, Once)
- Useful for: Tigre (Retiro → Tigre, 1 hr, scenic), La Plata (Constitución), Mar del Plata (Constitución, 5 hrs)
- Require SUBE card or separate ticket
- Generally safe inside trains and stations; exercise caution at Constitución at night

## Ferry to Uruguay

### Buquebus (Buenos Aires ↔ Uruguay)
Main ferry operator connecting Buenos Aires with:
- **Colonia del Sacramento**: ~3 hrs (slow boat) or ~1 hr (fast ferry — Seacat)
- **Montevideo**: ~2.5–3 hrs (fast ferry) or ~2.5 hrs (bus + fast ferry via Colonia)

**Terminal**: Puerto de Buenos Aires (Av. Córdoba y Alem; central; easy taxi/Uber access)

**Tickets**: www.buquebus.com — book ahead (weekends and July sell out); also at terminal on weekday mornings
**Price**: Around USD 50–100 return Buenos Aires–Colonia depending on class and season
**Documents**: Passport required (both Argentine and Uruguayan immigration stamped on board or at terminal)
**Note**: Colonia del Sacramento is a Portuguese-founded UNESCO colonial town — worthwhile as a day trip even if not continuing to Montevideo

### Cacciola and Líneas Delta Argentino (Tigre ↔ Uruguay)
- Slower, cheaper river crossing via Tigre Delta
- Tigre → Carmelo (Uruguay): ~3 hrs; good for Colonia/Carmelo wine region side trip
- Less comfortable than Buquebus but scenic river crossing through the Delta

## Patagonia Transport Notes

### General Principles
- Patagonia has vast distances; flying between anchor points + busing locally is the most efficient strategy
- Key air hubs: Bariloche (BRC), El Calafate (FTE), Ushuaia (USH), Río Gallegos (RGL), Comodoro Rivadavia (CRD)
- Long-distance buses in Patagonia are less frequent than in the north — book ahead

### El Chaltén Specific
- Only accessible by bus from El Calafate (3 hrs, scenic steppe road)
- Bus companies: Chaltentravel, Caltur — ~3 services per day in season; 1–2 per day shoulder season
- No airport; no ATM; no car hire
- Accommodation and food fully self-contained in town

### Carretera Austral (Chilean Patagonia)
- The famous gravel highway — only relevant if crossing into Chile
- Cruce de Lagos (Bariloche → Puerto Montt): 3-day scenic crossing through Andean lakes; fully organised by Cruce Andino operator; highly recommended; book weeks ahead

### Car Hire in Patagonia
- Useful for: El Calafate surrounds, Bariloche Circuito Chico, Mendoza wine valleys, Salta + Quebrada region
- Major companies at airports: Hertz, Avis, Budget, Localiza
- 4x4 recommended for El Calafate → El Chaltén road and some Salta mountain routes
- International driving licence required
- Gravel roads (ripio): Drive slowly; sharp rocks puncture tyres — carry a spare; some agencies charge extra for punctures

# Argentina Packing List Generator

## When to Use
- Preparing for a trip to Argentina (single region or multi-region)
- Building activity-specific gear lists (trekking, wine tours, urban)
- Packing for multiple climate zones in one trip
- Reviewing what documents and essentials to carry

## Procedure

### Step 1: Identify Regions and Season
Determine which climate zones apply and the travel month.
Consult [climate-zones.md](./references/climate-zones.md) for:
- Temperature ranges by region and month
- Rainfall / precipitation patterns
- Special conditions (Patagonian wind, Andean altitude, subtropical humidity)

### Step 2: Map Activities to Gear Categories
For each planned activity, identify gear needs:
- **Urban sightseeing** (Buenos Aires, Salta city): smart-casual clothes, comfortable walking shoes
- **Wine touring** (Mendoza, Cafayate): smart-casual, sun hat, sunscreen
- **Day hiking** (Nahuel Huapi, Tierra del Fuego NP): day pack, layers, waterproof jacket
- **Multi-day trekking** (El Chaltén, Aconcagua base): full technical gear, camp kit
- **Glacier activities** (Perito Moreno Mini Trekking, Big Ice): crampons provided — warm base layers + waterproof shell required
- **Iguazu / tropical**: lightweight clothes, waterproof bag for boat tour, insect repellent
- **Altitude areas** (Salinas Grandes, Tren a las Nubes): sunscreen SPF50+, lip balm, altitude sickness medication

### Step 3: Build the Packing List
Load [packing-checklist.md](./references/packing-checklist.md) for the full modular checklist organised by:
- Clothing (base, mid, outer layers)
- Footwear
- Documents & money
- Electronics & power
- Health & pharmacy
- Activity-specific gear
- Packing optimisation tips

### Step 4: Multi-Region Trips — Solve the Layering Challenge
When visiting both warm and cold regions:
- **Patagonia + Buenos Aires**: Pack Patagonia gear first; BA requires lighter layers — bring a packable down jacket that works in both
- **Iguazu + Patagonia**: Separate bags strategy — store BA/Iguazu items in hotel storage while in Patagonia
- **Northwest altiplano + Buenos Aires**: Layers for altitude (cold nights) + breathable clothes for Salta city days

### Step 5: Luggage Recommendations
- Domestic flights: 23kg check-in, 8kg carry-on (Aerolíneas Argentinas standard)
- LADE and regional charter flights: 15–20kg limits; verify when booking
- Long-distance buses: luggage stored in hold, no strict weight limit but be reasonable
- El Chaltén / El Calafate: 40L daypack sufficient if staying at guesthouses (no full camp kit needed for day treks)

## Adapting for Other Destinations
Identify equivalent climate zones at target destination; substitute regional names in climate-zones.md. The layering strategy and document checklist apply universally.

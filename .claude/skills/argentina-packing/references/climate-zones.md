# Argentina Climate Zones

## Zone 1 — Subtropical Northeast (Hot & Humid)
**Regions**: Puerto Iguazu, Misiones, Corrientes, Iberá Wetlands
**General**: Hot and humid year-round, high rainfall, lush jungle vegetation.

| Season | Months | Temperatures | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Summer | Dec–Mar | 28–38°C | Very humid, heavy rain, insect peak |
| Autumn | Apr–May | 22–30°C | Best weather — warm, lush, manageable |
| Winter | Jun–Aug | 15–22°C | Coolest, pleasant — good for visiting |
| Spring | Sep–Nov | 22–32°C | Water volume peaks for falls; warming up |

**What to pack**: Lightweight synthetics / linen, shorts, t-shirts, rain poncho, sandals + closed shoes, strong insect repellent (DEET 30%+), waterproof bag or dry sack (essential for boat tours), SPF50 sunscreen, after-bite cream.

## Zone 2 — Arid/Semi-Arid Northwest (Extremes of Hot & Cold)
**Regions**: Salta, Jujuy, Quebrada de Humahuaca, Salinas Grandes, Cafayate, Catamarca
**General**: Dry climate, strong sun, cold nights year-round. Altitude ranges from 1,200m (Salta city) to 4,500m (Salinas Grandes, Tren a las Nubes).

| Season | Months | Lowland Temp | Highland Temp | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Summer | Dec–Mar | 28–35°C day | 10–18°C night | Some rain (rainy season — roads can flood) |
| Autumn | Apr–May | 22–30°C day | 5–12°C night | Best time — dry, clear skies |
| Winter | Jun–Aug | 18–25°C day | -5–5°C night | Cold nights, dry, peak tourist season |
| Spring | Sep–Nov | 22–30°C day | 8–15°C night | Good; pre-rainy season |

**Altitude warning**: Salinas Grandes (3,450m), Tren a las Nubes (4,220m). Allow 1–2 days to acclimatise in Salta (1,187m) before ascending. Soroche pills (acetazolamide) available at local pharmacies without prescription.

**What to pack**: Layers (warm days, very cold nights), fleece or down jacket, light waterproof jacket, strong SPF50+ sunscreen, SPF lip balm, UV-blocking sunglasses, hat with brim, altitude medication, refillable water bottle (dehydration risk), light scarf (dust on roads).

## Zone 3 — Temperate Pampas & Atlantic Coast (Four Seasons)
**Regions**: Buenos Aires, Rosario, Córdoba, Peninsula Valdes, Mar del Plata
**General**: Classic four seasons. BA summers are hot and humid; winters mild. Peninsula Valdes is windier and cooler.

| Season | Months | Temperatures | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Summer | Dec–Feb | 28–38°C, humid | Hot; many locals on holiday |
| Autumn | Mar–May | 15–25°C | Excellent — comfortable, fewer crowds |
| Winter | Jun–Aug | 5–15°C | Mild; rain; coat needed but not severe |
| Spring | Sep–Nov | 15–25°C | Very pleasant; whale season for Valdes |

**What to pack**: Smart-casual clothes (Buenos Aires is fashion-conscious), light layers, packable rain jacket, comfortable walking shoes, one smart outfit for tango shows/upscale restaurants. Light summer dress/shorts Jun–Feb; sweater/light coat Jun–Aug.

**Buenos Aires dress code note**: Portenos dress up for dinner. Shorts at restaurants are uncommon. Pack one smarter outfit.

## Zone 4 — Patagonia & Andean South (Cold, Windy, Unpredictable)
**Sub-regions**:
- **Lake District** (Bariloche, San Martín de los Andes): Somewhat more sheltered, forested, alpine
- **Steppe & Glaciers** (El Calafate, El Chaltén): Open steppe, extreme wind, strong UV
- **Tierra del Fuego** (Ushuaia): Coldest, most changeable, sub-Antarctic

| Season | Months | Temperatures | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Summer | Dec–Feb | 10–22°C | Best trekking weather (still cold); very long days |
| Autumn | Mar–Apr | 5–15°C | Stunning foliage; fewer crowds |
| Winter | May–Sep | -5–8°C | Skiing in Bariloche; Ushuaia aurora possible |
| Spring | Oct–Nov | 5–15°C | Wildflowers; Patagonia season starts; windy |

**Wind warning**: El Chaltén and El Calafate experience gusts of 80–120 km/h routinely. Lightweight rain jackets will fail — a proper Patagonia/Arc'teryx-level hardshell is strongly recommended.

**What to pack for Patagonia (non-negotiable)**:
- Waterproof hardshell jacket (windproof + waterproof, taped seams)
- Insulating mid layer (fleece or down — down is lighter but useless when wet; merino fleece safer)
- Moisture-wicking base layer (merino wool preferred)
- Warm hat + buff/neck gaiter
- Gloves (waterproof outer shell)
- Hiking boots (waterproof, ankle support, broken-in before trip)
- Trekking poles (optional but helpful on steep sections)
- Daypack (25–35L) with rain cover
- Sunscreen SPF50 (UV very intense due to ozone thinning in southern Patagonia)
- UV-blocking sunglasses
- Water-resistant trousers or hiking pants

**For glacier activities** (ice trekking): Crampons, harness, and ice axes are provided by tour operators. Wear warm waterproof base layers + hardshell. Leave cameras accessible — waterproof case or dry bag for electronics.

## Multi-Zone Trip Planning

### Packing for Patagonia + Buenos Aires (same trip)
- Buenos Aires: smart-casual, lighter layers
- Strategy: Wear your bulkiest items (hiking boots, heavy jacket) on Patagonia flights; check or compress remainder
- Pack a versatile mid-layer (dark fleece or smart wool jumper) that works in restaurants AND on treks
- Avoid packing two separate wardrobes — most items should do double-duty

### Packing for Iguazu + Buenos Aires + Salta (Northwest circuit)
- Base: light summer clothes for Iguazu + BA
- Add: 1 fleece, 1 warm layer for cold Salta nights + altitude
- Key: Small compression bag for the extra layers; keep luggage under 23kg for domestic flights

### Packing for All Zones (Grand Tour)
- Use a 50–65L main pack + 25L daypack, or a 20" carry-on + small check bag
- Patagonia hardshell doubles as rain jacket everywhere
- Merino wool base layers regulate temperature in both cold Patagonia and warm Salta nights
- Leave heavy items at BA hotel if not needed for Buenos Aires portion

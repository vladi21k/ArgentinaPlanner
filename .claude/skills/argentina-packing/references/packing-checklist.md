# Argentina Packing Checklist

Modular checklist — check applicable sections for your trip. Items marked **(P)** are Patagonia-specific; **(T)** tropical/Iguazu; **(A)** altitude/Northwest.

---

## Documents & Money
- [ ] Passport (validity: 6+ months beyond travel dates recommended)
- [ ] Entry/onward ticket printout or digital copy
- [ ] Travel insurance documents (print + digital)
- [ ] Copies of passport photo page (physical + cloud backup)
- [ ] USD cash — small bills ($1, $5, $10, $20) for blue-rate exchange or emergencies
- [ ] Credit/debit cards (Visa/Mastercard — Amex acceptance limited)
- [ ] Argentine pesos (exchange on arrival or at official exchange houses — check current blue rate regulations)
- [ ] International driving licence (if renting a car)
- [ ] Yellow fever certificate (if arriving from Bolivia, Brazil, Paraguay — required)
- [ ] Travel health insurance card (keep accessible)

---

## Clothing — Essentials for All Zones

### Base Layers
- [ ] 3–5 t-shirts / tops (merino wool or quick-dry synthetic preferred)
- [ ] 1–2 long-sleeve base layer shirts (merino wool — warm and odour-resistant)
- [ ] 3–5 pairs underwear (quick-dry)
- [ ] 3–5 pairs hiking/multi-purpose socks
- [ ] 1–2 pairs comfortable everyday socks

### Mid Layers
- [ ] 1 fleece or wool jumper (can double as smart-casual in restaurants)
- [ ] **(P)** 1 insulating down or synthetic jacket (packable — essential)
- [ ] 1 smart-casual outfit (long trousers + shirt/blouse for BA restaurants, tango shows)

### Outer Layers
- [ ] **(P)** 1 waterproof hardshell jacket (windproof + waterproof — Gore-Tex or equivalent; non-negotiable for Patagonia)
- [ ] Rain poncho or packable rain jacket (adequate for Buenos Aires, Iguazu — lighter alternative)
- [ ] **(P)** Water-resistant trekking trousers / softshell pants

### Bottoms
- [ ] 2–3 trekking/walking trousers (convertible zip-off useful for multi-zone trips)
- [ ] 1–2 pairs jeans or smart trousers (BA urban use)
- [ ] 1–2 shorts **(T)** for Iguazu or BA summer

### Extremities
- [ ] **(P)** Warm hat (wool/fleece)
- [ ] **(P)** Lightweight gloves (or waterproof gloves for glacier activities)
- [ ] **(P)** Buff / neck gaiter (doubles as hat liner or face cover in wind)
- [ ] **(A)** Wide-brim sun hat (UV protection at altitude)
- [ ] UV-blocking sunglasses (essential Patagonia + altitude)

---

## Footwear

- [ ] **(P)** Hiking boots — waterproof, ankle support, broken in before trip (most important item for Patagonia)
- [ ] Comfortable walking shoes / trail runners (city + light trails)
- [ ] Flip-flops or sandals **(T)** (Iguazu, BA hotel, hostel showers)
- [ ] Smart casual shoes (BA restaurants, tango shows — optional but useful)

---

## Electronics & Power

- [ ] Smartphone (download offline maps: maps.me or Google Maps offline for Patagonia — no signal in parks)
- [ ] Camera + extra batteries (Patagonia cold drains batteries fast)
- [ ] Laptop / tablet (optional)
- [ ] Universal power adapter (Argentina uses Type I plugs — 3-flat-prong angled; same as Australia)
- [ ] Portable power bank (10,000+ mAh — useful in remote areas)
- [ ] USB-C and/or Lightning cable
- [ ] Waterproof phone case or dry bag **(T)** (Iguazu boat essential)
- [ ] Headlamp (for early glacier starts, camping, power outages in remote towns)
- [ ] Extra memory cards

---

## Health & Pharmacy

- [ ] Personal prescription medications (bring more than needed + copy of prescription)
- [ ] Travel vaccinations record (Yellow Fever if coming from endemic country — required)
- [ ] Ibuprofen / paracetamol
- [ ] **(A)** Altitude sickness tablets (Acetazolamide/Diamox — get from doctor before travel; or buy soroche pills in Salta pharmacies)
- [ ] Oral rehydration salts (altitude + heat dehydration)
- [ ] Antidiarrheal medication (Imodium)
- [ ] **(T)** Insect repellent with DEET 30%+ (Iguazu, Iberá wetlands, Corrientes)
- [ ] **(T)** Antihistamine cream / antihistamine tablets (insect bites)
- [ ] SPF50+ sunscreen (essential ALL zones — UV very intense in Patagonia and altitude)
- [ ] SPF lip balm
- [ ] After-sun lotion
- [ ] Blister prevention (Compeed plasters / moleskin)
- [ ] Hand sanitiser
- [ ] Small first aid kit (band-aids, antiseptic wipe, medical tape, elastic bandage)
- [ ] Motion sickness tablets (winding mountain roads in Mendoza, Salta, Bariloche)

- [ ] Daypack 25–35L (for trekking, day trips)

## What NOT to Pack

- **Don't bring** a cheap rain jacket for Patagonia — it will fail in 15 minutes in Patagonian wind
- **Don't bring** all new gear — break in hiking boots at least 2 weeks before departure
- **Don't overpack** smart clothing — Argentina is casual except Buenos Aires fine dining
- **Don't rely** on ATMs in El Chaltén (none) or remote Patagonia towns — carry enough cash
- **Don't bring** drone without registering with ANAC (Argentine aviation authority) — may be confiscated at borders

---

## Packing Optimisation Tips

1. **Roll clothes** — saves ~30% space vs folding
2. **Stuff socks inside shoes** — uses dead space
3. **Wear heaviest items on travel days** (hiking boots, thick jacket) — saves checked weight
4. **Leave 20% bag space** for souvenirs (leather goods, wine, textiles from north)
5. **Merino wool** — 1 merino t-shirt can be worn 3–4 days without washing; ideal for trekking
6. **Dark colours** for Patagonia — shows dirt less, wind hides grime
7. **Laundry**: Most Argentine cities and tourist towns have cheap laundry services (lavanderías) — pack for 5 days, wash mid-trip for 2+ week itineraries

# Argentina Regions Reference

## Buenos Aires
**Recommended stay**: 3–5 days (destination) | 1–2 days (gateway only)
**Best time**: Year-round. Avoid Jan–Feb (very hot, humid; many locals on summer holiday).
**Key neighbourhoods**:
- **Recoleta**: Elegant cemetery, MALBA museum, upscale cafés
- **San Telmo**: Sunday antiques market (Feria de San Telmo), historic milongas, cobbled streets
- **Palermo Soho/Hollywood**: Trendy restaurants, boutiques, weekend markets
- **La Boca / Caminito**: Colourful street art — stay within the tourist corridor; avoid side streets
- **Puerto Madero**: Modern waterfront, high-end parrillas, Ecological Reserve

**Top experiences**:
- Teatro Colón (one of world's top opera houses — book guided tours in advance)
- Tango show + milonga evening (Esquina Carlos Gardel, La Catedral, El Viejo Almacén)
- MALBA (Latin American art) + Bellas Artes (free)
- Recoleta Cemetery (free, self-guided)
- Football at La Bombonera (Boca Juniors) or El Monumental (River Plate)
- Tigre Delta day trip (1 hr by train/boat — unique river delta ecosystem)

**Advance booking**: Teatro Colón tours, popular parrillas (Don Julio, El Preferido, La Carnicería), tango shows.
**Day trips**: Tigre Delta (full day), Colonia del Sacramento Uruguay (ferry, full day — check visa rules).

## Mendoza & Wine Country (Cuyo)
**Recommended stay**: 3–4 days
**Best time**: Mar–May (harvest season — most atmospheric, cooler); Sep–Nov (spring blossom, less crowded); Dec–Feb (hot).
**Key areas**: Maipú, Luján de Cuyo (premium bodegas), Uco Valley (high-altitude vineyards).
**Top experiences**:
- Wine tasting circuit in Maipú (rent bikes or hire transfer)
- Catena Zapata, Achaval Ferrer, Zuccardi Valle de Uco (premium bodegas — book ahead)
- Aconcagua Provincial Park — Horcones Lagoon day hike (no permit for base walk); summit attempt requires months of prep + permits
- Potrerillos white-water rafting (Grade II–III)
- Villavicencio scenic mountain road (self-drive)
- San Martín Park evening walk + Mendoza city market

**Advance booking**: Premium winery tastings (Catena Zapata — weeks ahead), Aconcagua climbing permits (lodge reservations months ahead for summit bids).
**Pairs well with**: Buenos Aires (1.5 hr flight), Bariloche (1 hr flight).

## Patagonia: Bariloche & Lake District
**Recommended stay**: 3–5 days
**Best time**: Dec–Mar (summer trekking, wildflowers); Jul–Sep (skiing at Cerro Catedral); Sep–Nov (spring, fewer crowds).
**Top experiences**:
- Circuito Chico scenic drive (Punto Panorámico, Llao Llao Hotel area, Bahía López)
- Cerro Campanario viewpoint (chairlift — stunning panorama, sunrise recommended)
- Cerro Otto / Cerro Catedral trekking
- Nahuel Huapi National Park trails (free)
- Chocolate & craft beer town centre
- Cruce de Lagos: 3-day overland boat/bus crossing to Chile (Puerto Montt) — book weeks ahead

**Advance booking**: Cruce de Lagos crossing, mountain lodges in Jan–Feb.
**Pairs well with**: El Calafate (1 hr flight), Mendoza (1 hr flight), Buenos Aires (2 hr flight).

## Patagonia: El Calafate & Perito Moreno Glacier
**Recommended stay**: 2–3 days
**Best time**: Oct–Apr; glacier visible year-round but Nov–Mar has best weather.
**Top experiences**:
- Perito Moreno Glacier: free walkway circuit (2–3 hrs) + boat nautical safari (1 hr) + Mini Trekking (ice walk, 1 hr) or Big Ice (4 hrs, more challenging)
- Lago Argentino nautical tour (Upsala & Spegazzini glaciers — full day)
- Nimez Lagoon flamingo reserve (town, free at dawn/dusk)
- El Calafate town for evening asado

**Advance booking**: Ice trekking tours sell out weeks ahead in Dec–Feb. Book via Hielo y Aventura.
**Note**: ATMs unreliable — carry sufficient pesos from Buenos Aires.
**Pairs well with**: El Chaltén (3 hr bus), Ushuaia (1 hr flight), Bariloche (1 hr flight).

## Patagonia: El Chaltén (Fitz Roy Area)
**Recommended stay**: 2–3 days (weather-dependent)
**Best time**: Nov–Mar; weather is extremely changeable — plan flexibility.
**Top experiences**:
- Laguna de los Tres (Fitz Roy viewpoint) — 22 km round trip, full day, strenuous final ascent
- Laguna Torre (Cerro Torre view) — 18 km round trip, moderate, full day
- Mirador del Fitz Roy (short easy viewpoint, 1–2 hrs)
- Chorillo del Salto waterfall — 30 min walk from town (swim in season)
- Evening at La Cervecería craft brewery

**Critical notes**:
- No ATM in El Chaltén — bring all cash from El Calafate
- Trekking is completely free, no permits required
- Trailheads start from town — no transport needed
- All hikes are self-guided; register at Visitors Centre on arrival (mandatory)
- Weather windows often only a few hours — flexibility essential

## Patagonia: Ushuaia & Tierra del Fuego
**Recommended stay**: 2–3 days
**Best time**: Nov–Mar (summer, 17–19 hrs daylight, warmer); Jun–Aug (ski season, possible aurora australis).
**Top experiences**:
- Tierra del Fuego National Park (bus or hire car): Lapataia Bay, Bahía Ensenada, beaver dam trail
- Beagle Channel boat tour: Les Eclaireurs lighthouse, sea lion colony, Magellanic penguin colony (seasonal)
- Martial Glacier hike (chairlift + 1.5 hr walk to glacier remnant)
- Tren del Fin del Mundo (End of the World train — touristy but fun)
- Ushuaia city: maritime museum, Presidio prison museum

**Advance booking**: Beagle Channel tours (book day before minimum), accommodation (very limited supply — book months ahead in Jan–Feb).
**Note**: Southernmost city in the world. Hypothermia-level wind gusts possible any month; always pack layers.

## Salta & the Northwest (NOA)
**Recommended stay**: 4–6 days
**Best time**: Apr–Nov (dry season; roads passable); Jul is school holiday peak — busier & pricier.
**Top experiences**:
- Quebrada de Humahuaca (UNESCO): Tilcara (Pucará ruins), Purmamarca (Cerro de los 7 Colores), Humahuaca town
- Salinas Grandes salt flats (3,450m — acclimatise first)
- Cachi & Calchaquí Valleys: colonial village, Bodega Colomé, cacti-lined mountain road
- Tren a las Nubes (Train to the Clouds, 4,220m — advance booking essential, often sold out)
- Salta city: Plaza 9 de Julio, MAAM museum (Llullaillaco Inca mummies — extraordinary), San Bernardo convent
- Cafayate wine town (torrontés white wine — unique to region)

**Altitude warning**: Salta city is 1,187m. Quebrada sites reach 3,000–4,500m. Acclimatise 1–2 days before strenuous activity. Altitude medication (soroche pills) widely available.
**Advance booking**: Tren a las Nubes tickets (book months ahead — very high demand).
**Pairs well with**: Iguazu (1–2 hr direct flight), Buenos Aires (1.5 hr flight).

## Iguazu Falls (Misiones)
**Recommended stay**: 2 days (Argentine side) + optional 1 day (Brazilian side)
**Best time**: Year-round. Aug–Nov for maximum water volume. Apr–Jun ideal (lush, comfortable temperature, smaller crowds). Jan–Feb hottest and most humid.
**Top experiences**:
- **Argentine side** (2/3 of falls experience): Upper Circuit, Lower Circuit, Garganta del Diablo (Devil's Throat) — electrifying up-close views
- Macuco Safari boat ride — you will get soaked; waterproof bag for camera essential
- Iguazu Jungle Explorer jeep + boat combo
- Bird watching at dawn: toucans, parrots, coatis (friendly but can bite)
- **Brazilian side** (add-on, half day): Panoramic walkway — best for photography of full falls; confirm Brazil visa requirements for your nationality

**Logistics**: Argentine park entry + boat tours = separate tickets. Spend full day on Argentine side. Most visitors base in Puerto Iguazu town. Book boat tours at park entrance or the day before.

## Peninsula Valdes (Patagonia Atlantic Coast)
**Recommended stay**: 2–3 days
**Best time**: Sep–Nov (Southern Right Whale watching, peak: Oct); Jan–Mar (Magellanic penguins at Punta Tombo, 1 hr south); Mar–Apr (orca beaching attacks on sea lion pups at Punta Norte — extraordinary wildlife event).
**Top experiences**:
- Puerto Pirámides whale watching boat (Sep–Nov — Southern Right Whales breach beside boat)
- Punta Delgada sea lion & elephant seal colony (SE peninsula)
- Punta Norte orca spectacle (seasonal)
- Punta Tombo penguin colony (day trip, Jan–Mar, 180km south)
- Caleta Valdés elephant seals

**Base town**: Puerto Madryn (1 hr from peninsula gate). Fly Buenos Aires → Trelew (45 min).
**Advance booking**: Whale watching boats (Oct peak — book 1–2 days ahead minimum).
**Pairs well with**: Buenos Aires (short flight), El Calafate if doing Patagonia circuit.

## Routing Logic Quick Reference

### Classic Grand Tour (14 days)
Buenos Aires (3) → Iguazu (2) → Salta (3) → Mendoza (3) → Buenos Aires or fly out (0+)
*Optionally swap Salta for El Calafate if Patagonia preferred over Northwest.*

### Patagonia Circuit (10–12 days)
Buenos Aires (1) → Bariloche (3) → El Calafate (2) → El Chaltén (2) → Ushuaia (2) → Buenos Aires (1)

### Short Patagonia (7 days)
Buenos Aires (1) → El Calafate (2) → El Chaltén (2) → Bariloche (2) → fly out

### Wine & Culture (7 days)
Buenos Aires (4) → Mendoza (3) → Buenos Aires out

### Northern Focus (9 days)
Buenos Aires (2) → Salta (4) → Iguazu (2) → Buenos Aires out

### Wildlife (9 days, Sep–Nov)
Buenos Aires (2) → Peninsula Valdes (3) → Buenos Aires (1) → Iguazu (2) → Buenos Aires out

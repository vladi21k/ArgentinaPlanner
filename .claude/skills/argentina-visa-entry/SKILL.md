# Argentina Visa & Entry Requirements

## When to Use
- Checking visa requirements for a specific nationality
- Reviewing what documents to carry for entry
- Understanding passport validity rules
- Planning land border crossings (Chile, Bolivia, Uruguay, Brazil, Paraguay)
- Health certificate requirements (yellow fever)
- Understanding stay limits and extensions

## Procedure

### Step 1: Determine Nationality and Entry Type
Ask for:
- **Passport nationality** (country of citizenship)
- **Entry point**: Ezeiza (EZE) international airport, Aeroparque (AEP) domestic, land border, cruise terminal, or ferry from Uruguay
- **Purpose**: Tourism, business, remote work, long-stay

### Step 2: Check Visa Requirement
Consult [visa-requirements.md](./references/visa-requirements.md) for:
- Visa-free list (most Western countries, most of South/Central America)
- Countries requiring visas in advance
- Reciprocity fee status (historically applied to US, Canadian, Australian citizens — verify current status as policies change)
- Permitted stay duration (typically 90 days tourism)

### Step 3: Review Entry Document Checklist
Load [entry-checklist.md](./references/entry-checklist.md) for:
- Passport validity requirements
- Proof of onward travel requirements
- Health documents (yellow fever certificate from certain origins)
- Customs declaration requirements
- Minor/child travel rules (special authorisation from absent parent required)

### Step 4: Address Specific Entry Scenarios
Common scenarios requiring specific guidance:
- **Land border from Chile**: Most crossings straightforward; some mountain passes close seasonally
- **Ferry from Uruguay**: Buquebus (Buenos Aires ↔ Colonia/Montevideo); separate Uruguay entry/exit
- **Cruise ship entry**: Different procedures; confirm with cruise line
- **Stay extension**: Tourists can extend 90-day stay at Dirección Nacional de Migraciones in Buenos Aires (or exit/re-enter)
- **Digital nomad / remote work**: Argentina introduced a Digital Nomad Visa — check current application requirements

### Step 5: Flag Time-Sensitive Requirements
- Yellow fever certificate: Must be issued at least 10 days before travel if arriving from endemic country
- Health insurance: Not mandatory but strongly recommended (private healthcare can be expensive for foreigners)
- COVID / health requirements: Check current entry health requirements close to travel date (regulations change)

## Disclaimer
Visa rules and entry requirements change. Always verify current requirements on official government sources:
- Argentine Consulate/Embassy in your country
- Dirección Nacional de Migraciones: www.migraciones.gov.ar
- Your own government's travel advisory (e.g., FCDO, State Dept, DFAT)

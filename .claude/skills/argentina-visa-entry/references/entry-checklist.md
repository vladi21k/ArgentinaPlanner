# Argentina Entry Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare documents and requirements before departure.

---

## Essential Documents — All Travellers

- [ ] **Passport** — valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned departure from Argentina; no significant damage
- [ ] **Onward/return ticket** — proof that you intend to leave (immigration officers may ask; printout or digital copy)
- [ ] **Proof of accommodation** — hotel booking for first night (may be requested at immigration); hostels and Airbnb confirmations acceptable
- [ ] **Proof of sufficient funds** — bank statement or credit card showing ability to support your stay (USD 50–100/day guideline); rarely asked but good to have
- [ ] **Travel insurance documents** — not mandatory but strongly recommended; carry policy number and 24-hr emergency contact
- [ ] **Copies of all documents** — photograph and cloud-store your passport data page, tickets, insurance; carry 1 physical photocopy separately from originals

---

## Visa & Entry Fees

- [ ] **Confirm visa status** for your nationality (see visa-requirements.md)
- [ ] **Check reciprocity fee status** — was suspended for US, Canadian, Australian citizens but verify current status at www.argentina.gob.ar before travel
- [ ] If visa required: **visa approval letter + original visa** in passport

---

## Health Documents

- [ ] **Yellow fever vaccination certificate** (ICVP) — required if arriving from/via Brazil, Bolivia, Peru, Colombia, Venezuela, Paraguay, Ecuador, or African endemic zones; must be issued 10+ days before arrival
- [ ] **Routine vaccinations** up to date — Hepatitis A & B, Typhoid (recommended for adventurous eating), Rabies (if visiting remote jungle areas)
- [ ] **Prescription medications** — carry in original packaging with prescription copy; quantities must be reasonable (no more than 90-day supply)
- [ ] **Controlled substances**: If travelling with narcotics or controlled drugs (e.g., strong painkillers, ADHD medication), carry a letter from prescribing doctor in Spanish if possible

---

## For Minors (Under 18)

- [ ] **Travelling with both parents**: Minors' passports + parents' passports; some officers may request proof of parent-child relationship (birth certificate)
- [ ] **Travelling with one parent**: Notarised authorisation from absent parent (ideally apostilled and in Spanish)
- [ ] **Travelling with non-parent guardian**: Notarised authorisation from both parents + documentation of guardian relationship
- [ ] **Argentine citizen minors**: Migración requires specific *autorización de viaje* for travel without both parents — consult the Registro Civil

## Customs Declaration

On arrival in Argentina you will complete a customs declaration (AFIP form or electronic via app):

**Items to declare**:
- Cash above USD 10,000 (or equivalent in other currency) — must declare; carrying up to USD 10,000 is generally permitted
- Goods for commercial purposes
- Products of animal origin (meat, cheese, fresh produce — may be confiscated)
- Medication in excess of personal-use quantities
- Drones — must register with ANAC (Argentine civil aviation authority) before use; registration at www.anac.gob.ar

**What you can bring duty-free** (approximate — verify current limits):
- Personal effects: reasonable amounts of clothing, personal electronics (1 laptop, 1 camera, 1 tablet, 1 phone)
- Alcohol: 2 litres
- Tobacco: 400 cigarettes or 50 cigars
- Gifts: up to USD 300 value arriving by air; USD 150 arriving by land

**Biosecurity**: Argentina has strict agricultural biosecurity. Do not carry fresh fruit, vegetables, meat, or dairy products from other countries — they will be confiscated and may result in a fine.

## At Ezeiza (EZE) International Airport

- Argentina has two Buenos Aires airports: **Ezeiza/Ministro Pistarini (EZE)** — international; **Aeroparque Jorge Newbery (AEP)** — domestic and some regional international (Uruguay, Brazil, Chile, Paraguay, Bolivia)
- **Immigration queue**: Can be long; Mercosur nationals use a faster lane; non-Mercosur queue separately
- **Biometric**: Fingerprinting of non-Mercosur visitors; standard procedure
- **Currency exchange**: Official exchange desks at EZE arrivals — rates vary; better rates available at casas de cambio in Buenos Aires city
- **Transport to city**: Tienda León bus service (45–60 min to downtown, reliable, ~$20 USD); taxi/Uber (1–1.5 hrs depending on traffic, ~$25–40 USD); avoid taxis at departures level — use official remise counter or pre-booked app

---

## Entry at Land Border

- Carry original passport (not just photocopy)
- Both countries stamp you in/out — ensure both exit and entry stamps received
- Mercosur citizens may use national ID (cedula) at most crossings
- Some crossings close seasonally (Andean mountain passes Jun–Sep) or at night — check hours before travelling
- You may need to complete a migraciones form at some land crossings (paper form handed out on bus)

---

## After Entry: What to Keep

- **Entry stamp**: Keep your passport with entry stamp safe — required on exit
- **Tourist card (Tarjeta de Turismo)**: If issued (sometimes given at land borders), keep it — required on exit; losing it may cause delays and a fine
- **Receipt of any fees paid**: Keep in trip documents folder

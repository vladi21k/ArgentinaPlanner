Day-by-Day Itinerary

🏙️ Buenos Aires

Dec 9–10 · 2 nights

"The Paris of South America" — tango, steak, grand boulevards, and European-style architecture

↗ View on Google Maps

Day 1 · Wednesday, Dec 9 — Arrival

  • 05:40 — Land at Ministro Pistarini International Airport (EZE)
  • Immigration & customs: Allow 1–2 hours. Have accommodation address ready.
  • Transfer: Pre-book a remise (private car) or use the official taxi stand — ~45 min, ~$25–35 USD to Palermo/Recoleta. Avoid unofficial taxis.
  • Morning: Check in early if possible; otherwise store bags and get breakfast at a café.
  • Afternoon: Rest and recover. Stroll around your neighbourhood (Palermo or Recoleta).
  • Evening: First dinner at a parrilla (Argentine steakhouse). Order a bife de chorizo or ojo de bife and a bottle of Malbec. You have arrived.
Stay Hotel in Palermo or Recoleta
Tip Palermo = trendy bars/restaurants; Recoleta = elegant, central

Day 2 · Thursday, Dec 10 — Sightseeing

  • Morning: Walk from Plaza de Mayo → Casa Rosada → Buenos Aires Metropolitan Cathedral → stroll south through Montserrat to San Telmo.
  • Afternoon: Take a taxi to La Boca / Caminito — the colourful, Genoese-influenced neighbourhood with street art and tango dancers. (Do not walk there from San Telmo — take a cab and stay within the main tourist area.)
  • Late afternoon: Recoleta Cemetery — monumental mausoleums, final resting place of Eva Perón (Evita). Walk through the elegant Recoleta neighbourhood.
  • Evening: Optional tango dinner show (highly recommended — try Café de los Angelitos or El Viejo Almacén), or dinner in Palermo Hollywood.
  • Early night: You have an early flight tomorrow.
Stay Hotel in Palermo or Recoleta
Note Tomorrow's flight departs from EZE — budget ~1h 30min transfer to airport

🌊 Puerto Iguazú

Dec 11–12 · 2 nights

Wider than Niagara, taller than Victoria Falls — one of the world's great natural wonders

↗ View on Google Maps

Day 3 · Friday, Dec 11 — Travel + Argentine Side of the Falls

  • Morning: Flight EZE → IGR (~2 hrs). Hotel check-in in Puerto Iguazú town (~8 km from the park).
  • Afternoon: Visit Parque Nacional Iguazú (Argentine side):
    • Upper Circuit (Circuito Superior) — walkway above the falls
    • Lower Circuit (Circuito Inferior) — get close to the base
    • Garganta del Diablo (Devil's Throat) — the centrepiece: a 150m-wide horseshoe cataract. Stand at the railing as millions of litres pour past you. Unforgettable.
    • Optional: ecological train through the jungle between circuits
  • Evening: Return to Puerto Iguazú. Dinner — try surubí (river fish).
Stay Hotel in Puerto Iguazú town
Note Bring a change of clothes or rain poncho — the Devil's Throat will soak you

Day 4 · Saturday, Dec 12 — Brazilian Side

⚠️ Visa Check: As of 2025, Israeli passport holders do not need a Brazilian visa for short tourist visits. Verify this before travel. If anything changes, use this day for a second full day on the Argentine side.
  • Morning: Day trip to Foz do Iguaçu, Brazil (30-min drive; border crossing). The Brazilian side offers the legendary panoramic view of the entire horseshoe — the photo that's on every postcard.
  • Afternoon: Optional Macuco Boat Safari — a speedboat ride to the base of the falls. You WILL be completely drenched. Absolutely worth it.
  • Evening: Return to Puerto Iguazú. Dinner and relax.
Stay Hotel in Puerto Iguazú
Note Bring your passport for the Brazil border crossing

🐋 Puerto Madryn / Península Valdés

Dec 14–16 · 3 nights

UNESCO World Heritage Site — the world's finest wildlife sanctuary for right whales, elephant seals, sea lions and penguins

↗ View on Google Maps

Day 6 · Monday, Dec 14 — Travel + Punta Tombo

  • Morning: Flight AEP → PMY (~2 hrs). Pick up rental car at Puerto Madryn airport.
  • Afternoon: Drive south to Punta Tombo (~180 km, ~2 hrs). This is the largest Magellanic penguin colony in the world — approximately 1 million penguins in December! A boardwalk trail lets you walk right among them as they ignore you completely.
  • Evening: Drive back to Puerto Madryn (~2 hrs). Dinner by the waterfront.
Stay Hotel in Puerto Madryn
Distance Punta Tombo is 180 km south; allow a full 4–5 hrs for the visit + driving

Day 7 · Tuesday, Dec 15 — Península Valdés Full Day

  • Morning: Drive to Península Valdés (80 km, ~1 hr). Pay park entry fee at the gate. Head to Puerto Pirámides for a whale-watching boat tour — December is peak season for southern right whale mothers and calves.
  • Afternoon: Drive around the peninsula:
    • Punta Norte — colony of elephant seals and South American sea lions. December has massive animals and pups.
    • Caleta Valdés — more elephant seals along stunning cliffs. Watch for orcas offshore.
    • Keep eyes open for guanacos, rheas (ñandú), and Patagonian maras en route.
  • Evening: Return to Puerto Madryn. Dinner with fresh Patagonian seafood — try merluza (hake) or camarones (prawns).
Stay Hotel in Puerto Madryn
Tip Binoculars essential. Bring snacks — restaurants on the peninsula are limited

Day 8 · Wednesday, Dec 16 — Free Morning + Prep

  • Morning: Optional return to the peninsula, or explore Puerto Madryn seafront. The EcoCentro (marine biology museum on a cliff) is excellent if open.
  • Afternoon: Return rental car to airport. Rest. Pack for Ushuaia — you'll need warm layers even in December.
  • Evening: Last dinner in Puerto Madryn. Early night.
Stay Hotel in Puerto Madryn
Pack Wind/waterproof jacket, layers — Ushuaia is cold and unpredictable even in summer

🐧 Ushuaia

Dec 17–19 · 3 nights

The southernmost city in the world — "Fin del Mundo" (End of the World). Gateway to Antarctica.

↗ View on Google Maps

Day 9 · Thursday, Dec 17 — Travel + Tierra del Fuego National Park

  • Morning: Flight PMY → USH (~2.5 hrs). Pick up rental car at Ushuaia airport.
  • Afternoon: Drive west to Parque Nacional Tierra del Fuego (~12 km):
    • Hike the Senda Costera (coastal trail along the Beagle Channel) — easy to moderate, stunning views.
    • Walk to Lapataia Bay — the literal end of Ruta Nacional 3, the Pan-American Highway (Buenos Aires: 3,079 km). Photo stop essential.
    • Visit the End of the World Train (Tren del Fin del Mundo) station.
  • Evening: Return to Ushuaia. Dinner — order centolla (king crab) — the local specialty.
Stay Hotel in Ushuaia
Note December: ~18 hrs daylight. You will not run out of light

Day 10 · Friday, Dec 18 — Martillo Island Penguins + Beagle Channel

  • Morning: Boat tour to Isla Martillo (Martillo Island) — the only place in the world outside Antarctica where you can walk among king penguins on guided land excursions. Also Magellanic penguins in abundance. Book well in advance — capacity extremely limited.
  • Afternoon: Drive the scenic route along the Beagle Channel. Optional: visit the Presidio (old prison/museum) or Museo del Fin del Mundo.
  • Evening: Dinner in Ushuaia — try local lamb (cordero patagónico) or king crab again.
Stay Hotel in Ushuaia
Note Martillo Island tours: book 4–8 weeks ahead. Limited to ~20 people per excursion

Day 11 · Saturday, Dec 19 — Free Day + Pack Up

  • Morning: Choose your adventure:
    • Hike up Cerro Martial for panoramic views (~3 hrs round-trip, moderate)
    • Kayaking in the Beagle Channel
    • Lago Escondido / Lago Fagnano scenic drive east (~150 km round-trip)
  • Afternoon: Return rental car. Explore Ushuaia's artisan market — pick up Tierra del Fuego crafts, local chocolates, and the famous "End of the World" stamps for your passport.
  • Evening: Last dinner in Ushuaia. Early night.
Stay Hotel in Ushuaia
Note Flight to El Calafate tomorrow — no onward car rental needed there

🧊 El Calafate

Dec 20–22 · 3 nights + Dec 25 · 1 night (return)

Gateway to Los Glaciares National Park — home of the world's most accessible and spectacular glacier

↗ View on Google Maps

Day 12 · Sunday, Dec 20 — Travel + Orientation

  • Morning: Flight USH → FTE (~1.5 hrs). Check in to hotel in El Calafate.
  • Afternoon: Walk along the turquoise shore of Lago Argentino. Visit the Glaciarium (glacier museum) if interested.
  • Evening: Dinner in El Calafate — try Patagonian lamb.
💵 CASH WARNING: Withdraw a generous amount of Argentine pesos at an El Calafate ATM before heading to El Chaltén. El Chaltén has NO ATM and minimal card payment options. Bring more than you think you need.
Stay Hotel in El Calafate

Day 13 · Monday, Dec 21 — Perito Moreno Glacier

This is one of the top natural spectacles on Earth. Give it a full day.
  • Full Day: Glaciar Perito Moreno — 78 km west of El Calafate (take a tour or hire a remise):
    • The pasarelas (walkways) give you close-up views of the 5-km-wide, 74-m-tall ice wall for FREE after park entry.
    • Watch and listen for calving — cathedral-sized chunks of ice exploding into the lake. Happens constantly.
    • Book a guided ice trek: Mini-Trekking (~1.5 hrs on the glacier with crampons) or Big Ice (~5 hrs). Both require advance booking months ahead.
    • Optional boat tour: Some operators offer a zodiac tour approaching the glacier wall from the water.
  • Evening: Return to El Calafate. Celebratory dinner.
Stay Hotel in El Calafate
Book ahead Ice trek tours — 2–3 months ahead at minimum

Day 14 · Tuesday, Dec 22 — Lago Argentino Boat Tour + Rest

  • Morning: Lago Argentino boat excursion — sail past the vast Upsala Glacier and Spegazzini Glacier (ice walls rising 135 m above water).
  • Afternoon: Rest and prepare for El Chaltén tomorrow. Buy food/snacks for the bus, and withdraw your cash now.
  • Evening: Dinner and early night — the bus to El Chaltén leaves early.
Stay Hotel in El Calafate

⛰️ El Chaltén

Dec 23–24 · 2 nights

Argentina's trekking capital, population ~1,500 — home to Fitz Roy and Cerro Torre

↗ View on Google Maps

Day 15 · Wednesday, Dec 23 — Bus to El Chaltén + Laguna de los Tres

  • Morning: Bus from El Calafate to El Chaltén (~3 hrs, ~220 km via RN40). Buses typically depart 7–8 am. Book in advance.
  • Late morning: Arrive El Chaltén. Drop bags. Trails start right at the edge of the village — no entry fees, national park rangers give free orientation talks at the bus terminal.
  • Afternoon: Start the Laguna de los Tres trail — the iconic Fitz Roy hike:
    • 20 km round-trip, ~1,200 m elevation gain, 6–8 hours total
    • Payoff at the top: Fitz Roy (3,405 m) reflected in the turquoise glacial lagoon. One of the great views in South America.
  • Evening: Return to town. Dinner — El Chaltén has surprisingly excellent restaurants. Toast to the hike.
Stay Hotel or cabin in El Chaltén
Weather Patagonian weather is notoriously changeable. Check the morning forecast — reschedule if winds are severe

Day 16 · Thursday, Dec 24 — Laguna Torre + Christmas Eve

  • Morning/Afternoon: Hike to Laguna Torre for views of the needle-like Cerro Torre:
    • 18 km round-trip, moderate difficulty, 5–6 hours total
    • Alternative: Shorter hike to Chorrillo del Salto waterfall (~4 km round-trip) if legs are tired.
  • Evening: Christmas Eve (Nochebuena) dinner — many restaurants serve special menus. Toast with local craft beer (El Chaltén has a microbrewery).
Stay Hotel or cabin in El Chaltén

🍫 Bariloche

Dec 26–31 · 6 nights · NEW YEAR'S EVE! 🎉

"The Queen of Patagonia" — alpine lakes, Andean scenery, Swiss-style architecture, world-class chocolate, and one of Argentina's biggest NYE celebrations

↗ View on Google Maps

Day 18 · Saturday, Dec 26 — Flight to Bariloche + Arrival

  • Morning: Flight FTE → BRC (~2 hrs). Pick up rental car at Bariloche airport.
  • Afternoon: Check in to hotel. Drive to the iconic Civic Centre. Walk the Nahuel Huapi Lake waterfront and browse the famous chocolate shops — Bariloche is Argentina's chocolate capital.
  • Evening: Dinner by the lake. Welcome to the Lakes District.
Stay Hotel in Bariloche (pre-booked well in advance!)

Day 19 · Sunday, Dec 27 — Circuito Chico

  • Full Day: Drive the Circuito Chico (~60 km loop west of Bariloche):
    • Cerro Campanario — 35-min hike (or chairlift) to Argentina's most-photographed panoramic viewpoint.
    • Llao Llao Hotel — the legendary 1938 alpine hotel. Walk the grounds; have a coffee.
    • Puerto Pañuelo — beautiful dock with views back to the hotel and across the lake.
    • Lago Moreno viewpoints.
  • Evening: Return to Bariloche. Dinner.
Stay Hotel in Bariloche

Day 20 · Monday, Dec 28 — Cerro Catedral + Tronador

  • Morning: Drive to Cerro Catedral (~20 km south) — South America's largest ski resort in summer. Take the cable car up for 360° Andean views.
  • Afternoon: Continue south to Cerro Tronador (~100 km, ~1.5 hrs). Hike to the Ventisquero Negro (the "black glacier"). Drive through the gorge of the Manso River.
  • Evening: Return to Bariloche. A long day — well worth it.
Stay Hotel in Bariloche
Note The road to Ventisquero Negro is partly unpaved; AWD recommended

Day 21 · Tuesday, Dec 29 — Circuito Grande

  • Full Day: Drive the Circuito Grande (~250 km loop north):
    • Villa La Angostura (~80 km) — charming lakeside village. Excellent lunch stop.
    • Bosque de Arrayanes — unique forest of cinnamon-barked arrayán trees.
    • Return via Paso Córdoba with Patagonian steppe views.
  • Evening: Return to Bariloche.
Stay Hotel in Bariloche

Day 22 · Wednesday, Dec 30 — Free Day / Adventure Activities

  • Options: white-water rafting on the Río Manso (class III–IV), kayaking on Nahuel Huapi, hike Cerro Otto, fly-fishing.
  • Afternoon: Buy chocolates for gifts at home (Abuela Goye, Mamuschka, Del Turista are the famous brands). Last chance.
  • Evening: Pre-NYE dinner. Make a reservation somewhere special.
Stay Hotel in Bariloche

Day 23 · Thursday, Dec 31 — New Year's Eve 🎉

  • Day: Relaxed morning. Final walk or hike. Rest up for a big night.
  • Evening: NYE on the Bariloche waterfront — one of Argentina's biggest New Year's celebrations:
    • The lakefront Centro Cívico and plaza fill with tens of thousands of revellers.
    • Midnight fireworks over Nahuel Huapi Lake — mountains and water as backdrop.
    • Massive street party continues until dawn.
Stay Hotel in Bariloche
Note This is why you booked the hotel a year early. Enjoy every moment.

🗺️ Practical Tips

Money

  • Bring USD cash in good condition (no tears, pen marks, or heavy creases — Argentine banks will reject them). $100 notes preferred.
  • Use an official casa de cambio or your hotel for exchange. Avoid unofficial street changers.
  • ATMs have daily withdrawal limits; extract cash in stages.
  • El Chaltén: NO ATM. Withdraw before the El Calafate bus. Bring more than you think you need.

Weather & Clothing

RegionDec–Jan ConditionsEssentials
Buenos AiresHot, humid (28–35°C)Light clothes, comfortable walking shoes
IguazúVery hot and humid (30–38°C)Light clothes, rain poncho/waterproof

In Patagonia, weather can change from sunshine to rain to wind in 20 minutes. Always carry layers.

Safety

  • Buenos Aires: Use Uber, Cabify, or InDriver rather than street taxis. Avoid walking in La Boca after dark. Be alert in crowded areas.
  • Patagonian trails: Stay on marked paths. Tell someone your route. Carry water and snacks.
  • Sun protection: The UV index in Patagonia is surprisingly high. Use SPF 50+.

Language

  • Basic Spanish is very helpful outside Buenos Aires. Argentines speak a distinctive dialect with vos instead of tú.
  • Key phrases: ¿Cuánto cuesta? (How much?), Una mesa para cuatro (A table for four), La cuenta, por favor (The bill please), Sin hielo (No ice).

Flights & Booking

  • Book domestic flights on Aerolíneas Argentinas (most reliable), or JetSMART / Flybondi (cheaper but less reliable).
  • December–January is peak domestic travel season. Book all flights at least 3–4 months ahead.
  • Luggage limits on Aerolíneas: typically 23 kg hold + 8 kg hand luggage.

Visa & Entry

  • Argentina: Citizens of Israel, the EU, UK, US, Australia, and most Western countries enter visa-free for up to 90 days.
  • Brazil (Day 4): As of 2025, Israeli passport holders do not require a visa for Brazil short tourist visits. Verify this is still in effect before travel.
  • Passport validity: Ensure all passports are valid through at least July 2027 (6 months beyond Jan 4 departure).
  • Proof of onward travel: Have the Jan 4 departure confirmation on your phone.
  • Always verify current requirements at migraciones.gov.ar and your own government's travel advisory before departure.
